BACKGROUND

ICONIE 2026

ICONIE 2026 is organized to discuss the role of Islamic economics in addressing current issues related to social justice, economic inequality, and sustainable development globally.

With the theme "Global Debt Crisis and the Islamic Alternatives: A Roadmap for Just Financial Systems, this conference aims to explore how Islamic economic principles can contribute to reimagining global financial systems, mitigating debt dependency, and promoting sustainable development— particularly in vulnerable economies. The world is facing a mounting debt crisis of unprecedented proportions. Sovereign debt levels in both developing and developed nations have reached unsustainable heights, exacerbated by decades of interest-based lending, economic mismanagement, and global financial shocks. As countries struggle under the weight of high interest repayments, austerity measures, and dependency on external bailouts, millions are pushed into poverty, and public services are eroded.

The current global financial architecture, rooted in interest (riba), speculative transactions, and asymmetrical risk, has proven inadequate in fostering justice, resilience, and long-term sustainability. In this context, Islamic economics offer compelling alternatives grounded in ethical principles, risk-sharing, and socio- economic justice. Models such as profit-and-loss sharing (PLS), zakat, waqf, sukuk, and non-interest-based financing can provide viable pathways toward equitable debt resolution, real economic growth, and financial stability. These approaches emphasize fairness, accountability, and the prevention of exploitative practices, aligning economic activity with the broader objectives of human dignity and collective prosperity.

This conference will bring together scholars, policymakers, financial practitioners, and development experts to critically examine current debt challenges and propose Islamic alternatives that are both theoretically robust and practically implementable in a multipolar world.

IMPACT OF THE PROGRAM

Creating Meaningful Change
1

Advance scholarly understanding of the global debt crisis through the lens of Islamic economics.

2

Generate new academic research and policy frameworks on interest-free, ethical public financing.

3

Encourage interdisciplinary dialogue between Islamic economics, conventional finance, public policy, and development studies.

4

Provide actionable recommendations to policymakers and regulators on integrating Islamic debt alternatives into national financial strategies.

5

Stimulate innovation in Islamic financial products and services related to sovereign finance, including new sukuk structures and debt-relief tools.

6

Promote social justice and financial inclusion through tools like zakat and waqf as part of public finance strategies.
